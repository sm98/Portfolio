OVERVIEW

Summary


The state of Odisha has 16 different handicraft forms. Golden grass weaving is one of the emerging handicraft. The Government of Odisha is promoting artisans through its training programs and trying to establish an independent organisation for them. Training centers are being created throughout different districts of the state. SELCO foundation, a non-profit organisation has partnered with the Government of Odisha for designing such training centers. We through Frugal Design Lab collaborated with the SELCO foundation to research, and identify gaps and opportunities for design interventions in the whole process of golden grass weaving.

Process



Problem definition

Mindmap


We started our research work by interacting with the SELCO foundation. They organised a presentation to impart whatever knowledge they have collected until then. We started going through previously done researches to get an overview of the process. Later we created a mind map where we brainstormed different aspects of this handicraft form.



Objectives


As soon as we started jotting down our thoughts, we realised there are multiple asspects to look into so we deivided the task into four objectives:

• Researching about existing handicrafts and products
• Understanding the process of Golden grass handicraft
• Looking at the larger system
• Identify possible design interventions



Secondary research

Handicrafts of India


We tried to diverge our thinking as much as possible. After completing the mind map we started looking into each of the nodes to explore further. We researched more about Dyes, possible products, markets, pricing, schemes for handicraft artists, international handicraft communities, etc. It was difficult to get information about golden grass handicrafts as not much content has been put up online.



Market Research


Then we looked into handicraft products made by similar kind of materials.



Primary research

Field trip


We planned a 4 day field visit to Kendrapara district of Odisha to understand the system from ground level. We visited few training centers and SIDAC office at Bhubaneswar.



Interview


We interviewed a lot of trainees over the period of 3 days. They shared their experience of 6 month training program. We were facing difficulty in having conversations. Few women helped us in translation. They showed us the process of different weaving styles for creating different products.



Probe


We created probe activity booklet with different activities aimed to gather data. We had to ask them and fill it ourselves as many of the women couldnt write in Odia or any other language. We planned to ask children of the village about how their perspective about continuing the handicraft business. But we were not able to do it due to time constraints.



Process mapping


After coming back from Odisha we compiled all the probes, interview transcripts and notes to create a process journey map. In this map we noted down the different stages of the handicraft product right from the cultivation of grass to selling. We identified stakeholders at each and every stage. After that we included tasks done and pain points. Writing down pain points helped us to understand different opportunities for design interventions.



ecosystem mapping


We created a map of different stakeholders and how the current training program is being run.



Ideation

Brainstorming


We worked on few concepts of tools and technology that can help to aid the artisans. All of the research content and ideas were then handed over to the SELCO group for further research.



Reflection

Learnings


- This was my first collaborative research project.
- Initially I was afraid of traveling and interacting with people from totally different state. This research project really boosted my confidence.
- Though we had a lot of language barrier, we were able to communicate through gestures.



What would I do differently?


- Weaving a small product on our own from start to finish would have given us more insight.
- It would have been better if we could spend more time to gain more insights and perspectives.
- Prepare a plan B for each and every activity involving primary research for backup incase things go south.
